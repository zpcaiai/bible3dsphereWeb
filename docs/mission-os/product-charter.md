# Product Charter

## Mission and users

Mission OS helps individuals, churches, mission agencies, teams, mentors, safeguarding officers, local partners, and local leaders carry a mission journey from evidence-backed field understanding to responsible local ownership.

The core loop is: understand a field, discern with community, prepare a worker, approve and send through accountable organizations, serve lawfully, care for the worker and family, develop local leaders, transfer authority, and evaluate durable local fruit.

The north-star measure is the share of active initiatives that remain safe, locally accountable, evidence-backed, and on a verified path toward local leadership and reduced external dependency. Activity volume, conversions, content consumption, and AI output are not north-star measures.

## Scenarios and scope

The product supports domestic cross-cultural work, overseas work, diaspora or international-student work, and rear-base prayer, finance, training, and member-care support. The first release establishes governance and three voluntary pilots. The full version adds the complete field-to-transfer lifecycle.

## Non-goals

Mission OS does not declare a divine calling, replace a church or agency, automate final sending approval, infer religious identity, diagnose illness, provide legal or immigration conclusions, create cover identities, or optimize coercive conversion.

## Governing principles

Local churches and credible local leaders have priority in interpretation, design, decisions, resources, and succession. Material aid, employment, education, health care, or crisis support may never be conditioned on religious participation. Poverty, danger, trauma, or dependency may never be used as leverage. Organizations identify their faith commitments honestly.

Every external-worker plan includes authority transfer, dependency reduction, succession criteria, and a dignified exit path. Continued external control is not treated as success.
