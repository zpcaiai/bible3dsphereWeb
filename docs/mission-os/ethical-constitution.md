# Ethical Constitution

1. Religious participation is voluntary, specific, informed, reversible, and free of penalty.
2. General care and public-benefit services are operationally separate from faith exploration.
3. Aid, work, education, medicine, protection, or access is never exchanged for participation.
4. A faith-based organization does not hide its identity or purpose.
5. Behavior, location, aid history, or content use is not used to infer religious identity.
6. The system and its AI do not declare that a person has received a divine calling.
7. Fear, illness, disaster, poverty, immigration status, or trauma are not persuasion tools.
8. Other religions and cultures are described accurately and without stigma.
9. Local churches, partners, communities, and leaders shape decisions and can dissent safely.
10. Minors and vulnerable adults receive stricter consent, visibility, contact, and escalation controls.
11. Refusing or withdrawing from a religious activity cannot reduce ordinary care priority.
12. Human safeguarding review overrides growth, engagement, program, and AI objectives.
